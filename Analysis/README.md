# Analysis Phase 

## Phase Goal: 
- This is where you define the project requirements, scope, and objectives. Plus, you’ll be gathering business requirements, understanding the customer’s needs, and setting clear goals. This phase also includes initial cost estimates, time frames, and risk analysis.

## Analysis and Plan Steps Breakdown
- Requirement gathering: Collaborate with stakeholders, including customers, to gather detailed project requirements. This can involve interviews, surveys, and reviewing existing documentation.
- Feasibility study: Analyze the gathered requirements to assess the project’s viability. Remember to include technical, operational, and economic feasibility.
- Define objectives: Clearly define the project’s objectives and what you aim to achieve. All this should align with your business goals and your customers’ expectations.
- Scope definition: Outline the project’s scale and exclude unnecessary items. This way, you’ll manage project boundaries and avoid scope creep.
- Initial planning: Develop a high-level project plan that includes cost estimates, time frames, resource allocation, and potential risks. This plan serves as a roadmap for the entire project.

## Key Roles: 
Project Managers, Senior Engineers, Stakeholders, Business Analysts, Product Owners
